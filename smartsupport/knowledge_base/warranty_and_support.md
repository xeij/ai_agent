## What's NOT Covered

### Exclusions
- **Physical damage** from drops, impacts, or mishandling
- **Liquid damage** from spills or flooding
- **Electrical damage** from power surges (unless surge protector failed)
- **User modifications** that cause damage
- **Software issues** from viruses, malware, or user error
- **Cosmetic damage** that doesn't affect functionality
- **Theft or loss**

### Warranty Void Conditions
- Removal or alteration of serial numbers
- Unauthorized repairs by third parties
- Use of incompatible or counterfeit parts
- Extreme overclocking beyond safe limits
- Commercial use of consumer-grade products (without business warranty)

## Common Support Scenarios

### System Won't Boot

**Troubleshooting Steps:**
1. Check all power connections
2. Listen for POST beeps
3. Check monitor connection
4. Try booting with minimal hardware
5. Reset CMOS/BIOS

**If Still Not Working:**
- Contact support for advanced diagnostics
- May require RMA for motherboard or PSU

---

### Performance Issues

**Troubleshooting Steps:**
1. Check Task Manager for resource usage
2. Update graphics drivers
3. Check temperatures (CPU/GPU should be under 85Â°C)
4. Scan for malware
5. Check for Windows updates

**If Still Experiencing Issues:**
- Remote support can optimize settings
- May indicate hardware degradation (covered under warranty)

---

### Overheating

**Troubleshooting Steps:**
1. Check that all fans are spinning
2. Clean dust filters and vents
3. Ensure adequate airflow around PC
4. Reapply thermal paste (if comfortable doing so)
5. Check fan curves in BIOS

**Covered Under Warranty:**
- Fan failures
- Pump failures (AIO coolers)
- Thermal paste degradation (within first year)

## Preventive Maintenance

### Recommended Maintenance Schedule

**Every 3 Months:**
- Clean dust filters
- Check for Windows and driver updates
- Verify all fans are working

**Every 6 Months:**
- Deep clean interior (compressed air)
- Check cable connections
- Monitor temperatures under load
- Update BIOS if new version available

**Annually:**
- Replace thermal paste (optional, for enthusiasts)
- Check for firmware updates on SSDs
- Verify all components are functioning optimally

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does upgrading components void my warranty?**
A: No, as long as upgrades are performed correctly and don't damage other components. DIY upgrades following our guides are covered.

**Q: What if I spilled liquid on my PC?**
A: Liquid damage is not covered under standard warranty. However, our Accidental Damage Protection plan covers this.

**Q: How long does an RMA take?**
A: Typically 5-7 business days from when we receive the system. Advance replacement is available for critical components (1-2 days).

**Q: Can I get a loaner system while mine is being repaired?**
A: Loaner systems are available for workstation customers with on-site service plans. Contact support to arrange.

**Q: What if the same component fails multiple times?**
A: If a component fails 3 times within the warranty period, we'll replace it with an upgraded model at no charge.

**Q: Do you offer extended warranty after the initial warranty expires?**
A: Yes, you can purchase an extended warranty up to 30 days after your original warranty expires.
